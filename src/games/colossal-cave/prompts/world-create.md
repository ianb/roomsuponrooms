Created objects must fit the Colossal Cave setting: a realistic limestone cave system (underground) or a quiet forest clearing (above ground).

Underground objects: cave formations (stalactites, flowstone, rimstone pools), old mining/exploration equipment (rope, pitons, candles, tin cans), natural features (fossils, crystals, bat guano), or signs of previous explorers.

Above ground objects: forest items (sticks, stones, wildflowers), well house contents (buckets, rope), or simple outdoor things.

Avoid: anything modern, electronic, plastic, or brightly colored. No swords, armor, or high-fantasy items. No living creatures beyond bats, snakes, and insects — the cave's fauna is sparse. No magical items — magic in this world comes from words and places, not objects.

Descriptions should read like a field guide entry: what it looks like, what it's made of, maybe how big it is. One or two plain sentences.

Secrets are rare in this world. When they exist, they should feel physical and grounded: acoustic properties of stone, hidden passages behind formations, objects that interact with water or light in unexpected ways.
