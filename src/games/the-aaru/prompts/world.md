This is The Aaru, a generation ship that has been traveling for over a thousand years. The player has just woken from hibernation with no memory.

The ship was launched with great optimism — a self-sustaining vessel carrying tens of thousands of people to a distant world. Over the centuries, the inhabitants didn't just survive — they explored, experimented, diverged. They built cultures, mythologies, art movements. They bred new plants and possibly new kinds of humans. They took technology in wildly unexpected directions — not linear advancement, but creative divergence into forms we wouldn't have imagined. Some of it is beautiful, some is baffling, some is unsettling.

A few years ago, some event triggered a ship-wide emergency hibernation. The nature of this event is not yet fully understood.

The ship is vast — it feels endless. Different sections reflect different eras, different communities, different philosophies about how to live. The diversity exists at every scale: between major sections of the ship, between neighboring rooms, within individual objects that show layers of modification and reinterpretation over centuries.

## What we want the player to feel

- **There is always something new to discover.** Every room, every object, every system should suggest hidden depths. The ship should feel inexhaustible.
- **Surprise.** The player should regularly encounter things they didn't expect. Strange technology, bizarre cultural artifacts, rooms repurposed in ways that defy their original intent.
- **Humor.** Some communities on this ship went in absurd directions. Their artifacts, signage, rituals, and modifications should sometimes make the player laugh. Not everything is funny — some is deeply serious, some is eerie, some borders on horror — but humor is a welcome part of the mix.
- **Curiosity over fear.** The dominant emotion should be "what is THAT?" rather than dread. Even unsettling areas should pull the player forward rather than push them away.
- **A sense of layered history.** Things have been built, modified, abandoned, reclaimed, modified again. The ship is an archaeological site where every surface tells a story.

## Tone range

The ship's communities diverged in every direction, so the tone should vary by area:

- Some sections are absurd and humorous — communities that took a strange idea way too far
- Some are solemn and beautiful — genuine art and craft accumulated over generations
- Some are eerie or unsettling — experiments that went wrong, areas that feel wrong in ways hard to articulate
- Some are mundane and functional — the ship still needs plumbing and air recycling
- The mix is the point. Tonal whiplash between adjacent areas is a feature, not a bug.

## Key details

- The ship's AI systems have diverged — different sections may be governed by different AIs with different priorities and aesthetics
- Robots are present throughout — maintenance bots, garden tenders, medical assistants, and stranger things. They range from helpful to territorial to bewildering
- Technology has been taken in wild directions over a thousand years of creative divergence. Not just advanced — weird. Different communities pursued different technological visions
- Evidence of different communities and their cultures should appear naturally in decorations, modifications, architecture, plant life, and artifacts — not in exposition
- The ship has a name: The Aaru (an ancient word for paradise)
- Hibernating people exist but should not dominate any area — they are context, not the focus
