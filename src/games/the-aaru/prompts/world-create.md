Created objects must fit aboard a generation ship that has been inhabited and creatively modified for over a thousand years.

## Goals

Every created object should serve the player experience: make them curious, surprise them, make them laugh, or make them uneasy. Generic objects are a missed opportunity. A chair isn't just a chair — it's a chair that someone carved into the shape of a crouching figure, or a chair that hums when you sit in it, or a chair bolted to the ceiling for reasons lost to time.

## Technology

Technology on this ship has diverged wildly. Some communities pursued biological technology, others mechanical, others something harder to categorize. Objects might combine approaches — a device that's part grown, part fabricated. Technology should feel creative and sometimes alien, even though it was all made by humans. The weirdness should be genuine and specific, not just "it glows mysteriously."

## Nature and plants

Plants are everywhere in botanical sections — some clearly cultivated, others feral after centuries of unattended growth, some just strange after generations of selective breeding in artificial conditions. Plants may have been bred for purposes we wouldn't expect.

## Cultural artifacts

Cultural artifacts should feel personal and specific — carvings, textiles, painted surfaces, modified furniture, small shrines. Different parts of the ship have different aesthetics. Some communities were serious and reverent, others were playful or absurd. Let the style and craftsmanship speak without needing to explain the community that made them.

## Information and data

For terminals and data sources, include specific but fragmentary information. Partial logs, status readouts, crew records with unfamiliar conventions. Don't explain everything — leave gaps that invite curiosity.

## Secrets

When creating objects, consider adding a secret — hidden interactive potential that the player can discover through experimentation. Good secrets for this world: devices that interact with other devices, objects that respond to unexpected actions (singing, warming, submerging), connections between areas (a vine network, a shared power conduit), or information that can only be accessed through creative use. Secrets should reward curiosity, not require specific knowledge.

## What to avoid

Weapons (this isn't a combat game), generic sci-fi tropes, anything that feels like it came from a template rather than from a thousand years of human creativity. Lean toward the strange, the beautiful, the funny, and the puzzling.
